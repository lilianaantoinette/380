Course Description

ENGL 380 - Approaches to English Studies

An advanced course that covers research methods; approaches to literary, rhetorical, and pedagogical topics; critical and literary terminology; genre; and advanced skills in writing and analysis. Students will read and analyze a variety of texts, and particular emphasis will be placed on developing their close-reading skills.

Student Learning Outcomes

  • Demonstrate advanced writing processes and formulate thesis statements
  • Demonstrate working knowledge of literary terminology
  • Show comprehension of cultural and historical contexts of texts
  • Explain important critical movements and types of literary questions
  • Develop persuasive arguments through close reading
  • Locate and evaluate criticism and scholarship
  • Use criticism effectively and document sources properly (MLA style)
  • Analyze rhetorical strategies in criticism and scholarship
  • Synthesize non-literary cultural expressions with literary ones
  • Show awareness of different pedagogical methods

  • A notebook for class notes and annotations

Course Policies

Attendance

More than two unexcused absences will lower a student's final grade. Excused absences require documentation for illness, family emergencies, religious reasons, jury duty, or university activities.

Late Work

Late submissions incur penalties: less than 1 hour (5%), one class day (10%), two class days (20%), more than one week (max 50%). One 48-hour extension available once per semester (not for final essay or exams).

Academic Integrity

Plagiarism, self-plagiarism, or unauthorized use of generative AI will result in a zero and possible course failure. Limited AI use for minor tasks is acceptable but must be cited. All submissions checked through Turnitin.

Classroom Conduct

Respectful discussion is encouraged. Avoid inflammatory language related to gender, sexual orientation, age, race, culture, or religion. Disrespectful behavior will not be tolerated.
